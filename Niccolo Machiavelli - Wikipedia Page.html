Niccolò Machiavelli

Niccolò di Bernardo dei Machiavelli (3 May 1469 – 21 June 1527) was an Italian diplomat, author, philosopher, and historian from Florence. He is best known for his political treatise The Prince (Il Principe), written in 1513.

Biography

Born during the Italian Renaissance, Machiavelli worked for the Republic of Florence. After the Medici returned to power, he was dismissed and later turned to writing. His ideas about politics, power, and realism have made him one of the most influential political thinkers in history.

Major Works

  • The Prince – A guide on political power and strategy.
  • Discourses on Livy – A study on republics and classical history.
  • The Art of War – On military strategy and philosophy.

Political Philosophy

Machiavelli emphasized the use of practical and sometimes ruthless tactics to maintain power and stability. His name gave rise to the term "Machiavellian", meaning cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous in politics.

Legacy

His writings remain widely studied and debated. He is considered a founder of modern political science. Though often misunderstood, many believe his work was more about honesty in describing power, not cruelty.
