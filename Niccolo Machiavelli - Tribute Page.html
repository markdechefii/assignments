Niccolò Machiavelli

Biography

Niccolò Machiavelli (1469–1527) was an Italian diplomat, philosopher, historian, and writer. He is most famous for his political book The Prince, which is often seen as a guide to political realism and power.

Famous Works

  • The Prince (Il Principe)
  • Discourses on Livy
  • The Art of War

Legacy

Machiavelli's name gave rise to the term "Machiavellian", which refers to cunning, strategic, and ruthless political behavior. Despite this, many scholars believe his work was more about realism and truth than evil.
